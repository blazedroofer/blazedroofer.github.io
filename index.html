My Recovery Journey

I walked out of custody in my mid-thirties with no ID, no bank account, and a decade of active addiction behind me. I had burned every bridge and lost almost everything.

Today, four years later, I'm a full-time roofer in Ottawa earning a trade wage and building a life that actually holds. I navigated methadone, shelter protocols, OHIP-funded rehab, safe supply, and the messy reality of re-entering the workforce while in recovery.

That journey taught me what actually works — and what doesn’t.

What I Can Offer

I’m happy to help anyone who’s struggling — whether you’re the one in active addiction, or you’re a parent, partner, or friend who doesn’t know where to turn.

I can point you toward real Ottawa resources, explain how the systems actually work on the ground, and share the practical steps that helped me get stable and stay working.

No judgment. Just honest guidance from someone who’s been there.

What I Believe

Harm reduction kept me alive. At the same time, I believe we need better structured, time-limited compassionate support to help people stabilize and regain their ability to choose.

Not punishment — just a practical reset so they can actually rebuild.
