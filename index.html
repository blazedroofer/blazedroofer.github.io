My Story

I walked out of custody in my mid-thirties with no ID, no bank account, and a decade of addiction behind me.

Today I'm a full-time roofer in Ottawa earning a trade wage. Four years into building a life that actually holds.

What Recovery Actually Looks Like

I write about the real, often boring basics that moved the needle — the Ottawa systems I navigated (methadone, shelter protocols, OHIP-funded rehab, safe supply), and what Canada's addiction response gets right and gets wrong.

What I Believe

Harm reduction kept me alive. At the same time, I believe we need better structured options — time-limited, compassionate support that helps people stabilize and regain their capacity to choose.

Not punishment. Just a practical reset so they can actually rebuild.
