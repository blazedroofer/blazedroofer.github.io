Cold Turkey Is Dead: Why Medical Detox Is the Only Safe Move in Ontario’s Toxic Drug Supply

I used to think cold turkey was just part of the price.

Lock yourself in a room. Sweat through your clothes. Puke into a bucket. Shake, cry, and white-knuckle it for a few days. A lot of us wore that pain like a badge — the tougher you were, the more you earned your recovery.

That thinking will get you killed now.

Back in 2011 when I first hit detox in Kitchener, a lot of people were coming off oxys and hydros. It was ugly as hell, but it felt more straightforward.

The street supply today is a completely different animal — fentanyl, street benzos, xylazine, medetomidine, and whatever else they throw in. People aren’t just getting sick when they try to quit. They’re seizing, hallucinating, ending up in delirium, or dead.

I’m a roofer in Ottawa, four years clean, and I’m telling you straight: cold turkey is dead.

The New Reality

I tried to quit cold turkey once after about seven months of everyday IV use. Seizure. Girlfriend got hit trying to help. Woke up in the back of a police cruiser and got charged.

Last time in jail I went into full psychosis. Too scared to tell anyone. Got paranoid, got beaten up, thought I was riding a motorcycle around the hospital, woke up shackled to a gurney for a week.

A buddy tried it too. Found him incontinent in a rooming house, deep in delirium. Barely made it.

What Medical Detox Actually Is (and Why the Current System Falls Short)

Medical detox is the only safe move. But in Ontario, safe does not always mean easy to access.

Many places are non-hospital, abstinence-based. They limit movement and ask you to sit still in a bubble while your body clears out. With today’s stacked unregulated chemicals, that’s not always realistic.

The Bed Problem Nobody Wants to Talk About

Some places are doing their best with limited beds, on-call doctors, and people way sicker than the system was built for. If you’re seizing or in psychosis, they may have to redirect you to emergency. That doesn’t mean detox is bullshit — it means the toxic supply has outpaced the system.
