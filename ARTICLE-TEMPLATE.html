Opening paragraph. Set the scene in 2–3 sentences. What's happening, what's the problem, why does it exist.

Second paragraph. Don't bury the point. The single most important sentence in this paragraph goes bold like this. Keep it moving.

Your First Section Heading

Body text. 3–4 lines then break. Write the way you talk — not like a report.

"The most powerful sentence in this section. One line. No hedging."

Follow-up paragraph. What does that mean in practice? What did you see? What does the reader need next?

  • First point — 1–2 lines, specific and concrete
  • Second point — something that surprises or reframes
  • Third point — grounded in your actual experience
  • Fourth point if needed

Your Third Section Heading

By now the reader is invested. Go deeper. If there's a sentence that captures the whole section, bold it.

  1. First step or stage — what does this actually look like
  2. Second step — what changes, what happens next
  3. Third step — be specific, not general
  4. Fourth if needed

What This Means for You

End on the specific, not the abstract. Who does this affect? What can someone actually do with this?

  • One concrete thing to know or do
  • Second concrete takeaway
  • Something they can hold onto or share
